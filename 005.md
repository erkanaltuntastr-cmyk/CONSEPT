# Oakwood – Brand & Naming Foundations

## Scope

This document captures the **initial branding and company naming direction** for Oakwood.
It is the first canonical reference for marketing-level decisions. Nothing here is accidental.

Owner: Managing Director (Erkan Altuntaş)
Custodian: Company Secretary / Business Partner (AI)

---

## Brand Positioning (High-Level)

Oakwood is positioned as a **serious, long-term, UK-oriented product company**, not a hobby project or short-lived startup.

The brand must:

* Support multiple products and verticals (Education, Health, Tracking, Platforms)
* Remain valid even if individual concepts (e.g. Easy Tracking) evolve or are replaced
* Sound credible to parents, schools, partners, and paying customers

---

## Accepted Naming Directions

The following suffixes are explicitly approved and aligned with the Oakwood vision:

### 1. Oakwood Labs

**Meaning:**

* Product-first
* Experimentation with discipline
* Innovation without chaos

**Implications:**

* Suitable for early-stage to growth-stage
* Strong fit for Easy Tracking as a flagship concept
* Allows future Labs-style products under one roof

**Tone:** Modern, technical, thoughtful

---

### 2. Oakwood Studio

**Meaning:**

* Design-led
* Human-centred products
* Craft and usability focus

**Implications:**

* Strong for UX-driven education and family-facing products
* Slightly more boutique
* Less corporate, more creative

**Tone:** Calm, premium, approachable

---

### 3. Oakwood Systems

**Meaning:**

* Reliability
* Infrastructure
* Long-term thinking

**Implications:**

* Best for B2B, platforms, or multi-product ecosystems
* Extremely future-proof
* Less emotional, more institutional

**Tone:** Serious, robust, enterprise-ready

---

### 4. Oakwood Digital

**Meaning:**

* Clear digital focus
* Broad but safe

**Implications:**

* Least opinionated
* Works well as a legal or umbrella entity
* Less distinctive as a product brand

**Tone:** Neutral, corporate, safe

---

## Initial Directional Insight (Not Final)

At this stage:

* **Labs** and **Systems** offer the strongest balance between flexibility and credibility
* **Studio** is compelling if design and human experience are positioned as the main differentiator
* **Digital** is acceptable but not emotionally strong

No final selection has been made yet. This document records the **approved option set**, not a decision.

## Brand Metaphor System – Applied Decisions

### Core Rule (Locked)

**Metaphors are narrative tools, not customer identities.**
They must never weaken trust, pricing power, or perceived seriousness.

---

### Acorn – Approved Usage (External, Controlled)

**Acorn represents the *starting phase*, not the person.**

Approved uses:

* Campaign language ("Start with an acorn-sized step")
* Onboarding storytelling ("Every oak starts as an acorn")
* Trial / non-paying phase narrative (copy only, never labels)
* Content, blog, manifesto, landing pages

Explicitly forbidden:

* Replacing the word *customer*, *user*, or *member*
* Pricing tiers named "Acorn"
* Support, billing, legal, or transactional language

---

### Trial vs Paid – Growth Language (Not Labels)

We describe *progress*, not people.

* Trial phase: "planting", "first acorns", "early growth"
* Paid phase: "growing roots", "established routines"
* Long-term users: "branches forming", "part of the forest"

No user is ever *called* an acorn, seed, or tree.

---

### Campaign Concepts (Approved)

* "Plant Your First Acorn" (trial onboarding)
* "From Acorn to Routine" (conversion campaign)
* "Grow Roots That Last" (retention)

Campaign metaphors must always map to **clear business actions** (trial → paid → retained).

---

### Forest Narrative – High-Level Brand Story

Oakwood positions its ecosystem as a **forest**:

* Independent growth
* Shared environment
* Strength through coexistence

Usage rules:

* Forest language allowed in vision, manifesto, sustainability messaging
* Not used in dashboards, billing, or performance metrics

---

### Tree Planting Initiative (Conditional Approval)

Concept: planting a real tree/sapling on behalf of customers.

Approved **only if**:

* Directly tied to paid milestones (e.g. annual subscription)
* Communicated as a *symbolic action*, not virtue signalling
* Executed via a credible UK/EU partner

This is a **Phase 2+ initiative**, not MVP.

---

### Internal Language (Unchanged)

* Roots = core model / code
* Branches = product lines
* Leaves = services / features

Internal language never leaks into customer-facing transactional flows.
