# Oakwood – Business Model & Revenue Logic

## Financial Philosophy (Locked)

Oakwood is **founder‑funded** at launch.

* Initial investor: Managing Director (internal)
* Launch cost: **near‑zero by design**
* Capital injection increases **only after real demand and usage are proven**

This is a **capital‑efficient, patience‑driven model**, not a burn‑first startup.

Oakwood does not depend on advertising revenue, attention harvesting, or dark‑pattern monetisation.

---

## Who Pays?

### 1. Individuals (End Users)

**Who:** Families, parents, individuals managing daily life, education, health, or routines.

**How they pay:**

* Minimal, trust‑based pricing
* Example: £3.99 per year (or lower at launch)

**Principle:**

* Payment is symbolic as well as financial
* Reinforces trust and commitment
* No ads, no selling user attention

---

### 2. Institutions (Primary Revenue Driver)

**Who:**

* Schools and education groups
* Gyms and sports organisations
* Automotive / service chains (e.g. large multi‑site operators)
* Any organisation responsible for **large numbers of people and recurring data**

**How they pay:**

* Licensing
* Platform access
* Custom integrations
* White‑label or semi‑branded deployments

This is where **meaningful revenue** is expected.

---

## What Do They Pay For?

Institutions pay for:

* Structured, clean, AI‑ready data
* Reduced operational friction
* Clear communication with many users
* A system that formats information instead of overwhelming people

End users pay for:

* Calm, ad‑free experience
* Simplicity and trust
* Tools that save time instead of consuming it

---

## Why Now?

* AI adoption is accelerating, but **most models are still generic**
* Organisations struggle to apply AI meaningfully to human‑scale, daily data
* Users are overwhelmed by dashboards, notifications, and complexity

Oakwood positions itself **between raw data and AI models**:

> A human‑first layer that structures, filters, and formats information before it reaches AI.

By the time AI models become fully specialised, Oakwood already owns:

* the behaviour
* the structure
* the trust

---

## Why Oakwood?

Oakwood is built by people **inside the problem**:

* Parents
* Families
* Individuals managing real routines

Not:

* Isolated developers
* Corporate innovation labs
* Abstract productivity theorists

This lived experience shapes:

* product decisions
* feature rejection
* language and tone

Oakwood does not optimise for engagement.
Oakwood optimises for **usefulness and longevity**.

---

## Long‑Term Positioning

Oakwood is not a single app.

It is a **model** that can be applied wherever:

* information is frequent
* users are many
* clarity is missing

The goal is not to monetise attention.
The goal is to become a **trusted infrastructure partner**.

---

## Revenue Rules (Non‑Negotiable)

* No ads
* No selling user data
* No artificial friction to upsell
* No features added solely to increase engagement

If revenue requires breaking trust, the revenue is rejected.
